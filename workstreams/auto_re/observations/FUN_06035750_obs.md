## Overview

FUN_06035750 is called as sub-function #11 from the player physics dispatcher
(FUN_0604D380). It manages a chain of 16-bit countdown timers at GBR+0x182
through +0x18C, with speed and steering threshold gates controlling activation.
Also performs velocity magnitude computation (sum-of-squares + sqrt) and
conditional writes to +0xC4, +0xF8, +0xD8.

## Timer Chain Structure

The function manages 6 consecutive 16-bit countdown timers at GBR+0x182:

| Offset | Timer # | Effect when counter reaches 0 |
|--------|---------|-------------------------------|
| +0x182 | 0 | Controls counter-based gating (velocity sqrt block) |
| +0x184 | 1 | Set to 10 or 30 by steering/speed conditions |
| +0x186 | 2 | Clears +0x18E to 0 |
| +0x188 | 3 | Decays +0xF8 by multiplying with 0x000107AE |
| +0x18A | 4 | Sets +0xF8 to constant 0x09D58000 |
| +0x18C | 5 | Halves +0xF8, conditionally negates +0xC4 into +0xD8 |

All timers are -1 (inactive) at idle from dead stop. They activate when
speed and steering thresholds are met.

## Other Observations

- The velocity magnitude computation uses sum-of-squares of three components
  (+0xF8², +0xEC², +0xC4²) followed by a call to FUN_0604818C (likely integer
  sqrt). The result is clamped and written back to +0xC4.
- Timer #3 decays +0xF8 by ~6.5% per frame (multiply by 0x000107AE ≈ 1.03
  in fixed-point, but the result is written as 16-bit so this is actually
  a slow decay). Timer #4 resets +0xF8 to a large constant.
- The steering threshold of 0xE00 (3584) combined with speed > 100 km/h
  activates the counter increment — this likely triggers drift/slide state
  transitions at high speed with significant steering input.
- Helper FUN_06035624 (from same TU) is NOT called from this function —
  it's called by FUN_060354A0 only.
