## Other Observations

- FUN_0604D580 and its continuations (0x0604D6B8, 0x0604D658, 0x0604D758, 0x0604D780)
  are called sequentially from the dispatcher. They share the same GBR context and
  operate on overlapping field ranges (+0x78 through +0x9C, and +0x24/+0x34).
- Code flow: reads fields that change with input → clamps/scales → writes derived fields.
- FUN_06048160 (at 0x06048160) is called as a math helper via r12. Used in both
  the +0x78→+0x7C path and the +0x24→+0x34 path.
- The `.4byte sym_046E0626` at line 392 is an encoded instruction in the byte fog,
  likely `mov.l r6, @(r0, r4)` — the GBR+0x7C write instruction.
- The +0x34 output is clamped to 0x14E (334 decimal) maximum.
- The +0x88/+0x98 fields use a range of [0x38, 0xB8] — 0x38 when idle, 0xB8 at
  saturation. The delta (0x80 = 128) is then scaled by 0xFF and divided to produce
  the +0x8C/+0x9C outputs.
